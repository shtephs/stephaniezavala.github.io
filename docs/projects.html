Research Publications

One Health in Social-Ecological Systems: Optimizing Natural Resources, Livelihoods and Health of Humans and Animals in Mongolia

Status: Under revision | Role: Co-author, Annex Development

This interdisciplinary research explores the complex relationships between human communities, livestock, wildlife, and environmental health in Mongolia’s pastoral systems. My contribution focused on developing comprehensive annexes that bridge veterinary science with socio-ecological analysis.

Key Contributions: - Development of technical annexes linking animal health indicators to ecosystem sustainability - Integration of veterinary epidemiological concepts with social-ecological frameworks - Synthesis of livestock health data with environmental and livelihood outcomes

Current Research

Master’s Thesis: Environmental and Anthropogenic Factors for Tick-borne Diseases in Switzerland

Status: Protocol development phase | Expected completion: 2026

Investigating how climate change and human activities influence the distribution and risk patterns of tick-borne diseases across Swiss landscapes.

Methodology: - Time series analysis using national surveillance data - Geographic Information Systems (GIS) for spatial analysis - Integration of environmental, demographic, and epidemiological datasets - Statistical modeling to identify risk factors and predict future trends

Data Sources: Swiss national health surveillance systems and meteorological data

Technical Projects

Mathematical Modeling of Influenza Epidemics

Tools: R, SEIR models, epidemiological modeling

Developed comprehensive mathematical models to simulate influenza transmission dynamics, combining SEIR (Susceptible-Exposed-Infected-Recovered) frameworks with additional epidemiological parameters.

Technical Skills Demonstrated: - Advanced R programming for epidemiological modeling - Parameter estimation and model validation - Scenario analysis and sensitivity testing - Data visualization of epidemic curves and transmission patterns

Research Data Management & Analysis

Tools: R, data cleaning, statistical analysis

Comprehensive data cleaning and analysis project focusing on research data management best practices. Worked with complex, real-world datasets to demonstrate proficiency in: - Data quality assessment and cleaning protocols - Statistical analysis pipeline development - Documentation and reproducible analysis workflows - Data visualization and reporting

Innovation in Development

VetClinic Assistant App

Status: In development | Target: Mexican veterinary market

Developing an AI-powered mobile application to revolutionize veterinary consultations by reducing administrative burden and improving clinical documentation accuracy.

Key Features: - Audio-to-text transcription of veterinary consultations - Automated consultation summaries with key clinical findings - Diagnostic suggestions based on clinical protocols - Streamlined medical records management - Time optimization for veterinary professionals

Technical Stack: Mobile app development, natural language processing, veterinary clinical databases

Impact Goal: Reduce consultation documentation time by 50% while improving record accuracy and clinical decision support for veterinarians in Mexico.

Skills Demonstrated Across Projects

  • Programming: R, statistical modeling, data visualization
  • Analysis: Time series analysis, GIS, epidemiological modeling
  • Research: Literature review, protocol development, interdisciplinary collaboration
  • Innovation: Mobile app development, AI integration, user experience design
  • Communication: Scientific writing, technical documentation, cross-cultural collaboration
